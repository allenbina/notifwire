# Philosophy

## Your notifications are yours

notifwire exists because of a simple belief: the alerts your devices generate
belong to you, not to the platforms that send them.

A notification is a signal about *your* life — a code you need, a job that
finished, a payment that posted, a person trying to reach you. Today those
signals are scattered across devices and locked inside apps. notifwire gathers
them onto hardware you control and lets you decide what happens next.

## Self-hosting is freedom

When you self-host, you answer to no one. No terms of service changes. No
surprise feature removals. No data mining. No accounts to create. There is no
central notifwire server — every install is a node, and any always-on node can
be the hub. You run the software, you own the data, you make the rules.

## Simplicity over features

notifwire does one thing well: it captures notifications and routes them where
you want, natively. It resists becoming a bloated platform. Small, sharp tools
that do one job and compose well — that's the Unix way, and it's our way. The
`notifwire-send` CLI is a single binary any script can call; plugins are plain
processes that speak HTTP. No SDK required.

## Open source, always

notifwire is MIT licensed and will always be open source. You can read every
line of code that touches your notifications. No black boxes, no hidden
behavior, no trust required — verify it yourself.

## Privacy is not negotiable

notifwire collects nothing. No telemetry, no analytics, no phone-home. Transport
is TLS everywhere, and end-to-end encryption is built in for sensitive content
like 2FA codes and financial alerts — the hub routes ciphertext it cannot read.
The software runs on your hardware and talks only to the services you explicitly
configure. Your privacy is the default, not a setting.
